Training Environment

Virtual worlds provide a dynamic collaborative learning environment that would otherwise be too expensive, dangerous, and/or impossible to implement in real world. Virtual worlds can create complex simulations and/or dynamic team interactions, enhancing training in sales, customer service, new hire on-boarding, compliance, leadership, and/or military training.

Your current LMS can be easily integrated directly into the virtual environment or pro grammatically. Virtual worlds bring trainees and their instructors into the same virtual workspace where they can ’feel’ as if they inhabit the same immersive environment. Simulate Business Scenarios and Prototype New Products

Virtual worlds enable participants from distributed locations to prototype products, simulate business situations, and rehearse training scenarios-all in real-time.

Creating prototypes in virtual worlds can have many advantages. Avoid the time and expense of creating physical prototypes at the beginning phases of product concept and design and instead build the product inworld. Bring teams together to walk around it, manipulate it, get inside of it, and experience it, providing a more informed and immediate feedback loop that can be incorporated directly into the design, on the fly.

Meeting or Event

Disparate teams can come together for meetings in a shared immersive environment to present, brainstorm, and make decisions. With the persistent nature of virtual worlds, teams can come back to a virtual project room to continue a conversation and meeting rooms can support collaboration long after the official meeting has ended. Interactions within the environment can happen via spatial voice or text chat. Collaboration with content can happen in a much more dynamic way—in a virtual world, users can give presentations, share desktops and navigate the web all while remaining visually connected to one another.
